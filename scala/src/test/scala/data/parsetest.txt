'''''28 Days Later: The Soundtrack Album''''' is the accompanying [[soundtrack album|soundtrack]] to the [[2002 in film|2002 film]] ''[[28 Days Later]]''. It was released on June 17, 2003. The [[Film score|original score]] was composed by [[John Murphy (composer)|John Murphy]], and tracks from [[Brian Eno]], [[Grandaddy]] and [[Blue States (band)|Blue States]] which featured in the movie also appear on the album.  The second movement of "[[East Hastings]]" by the Canadian [[post-rock]] band [[Godspeed You! Black Emperor]], albeit condensed, appeared in the movie but not on the soundtrack album.

==Reception and single releases==
"In the House - In a Heartbeat" is an [[instrumental]] track by [[John Murphy (composer)|John Murphy]]. The track was featured over the climactic confrontation of the film, and recurs in several scenes in the sequel, [[28 Weeks Later]]. It is also featured in a climactic torture and fight scene in 2010's [[Kick-Ass_(film)|Kick-Ass]], and was covered by British [[Death Metal]] band [[The Rotted]] on their album 'Get Dead Or Die Trying'. It was also featured in a trailer for the post-apocalyptic Russian videogame ''[[Metro 2033]]''.

"In Paradisum", a song arranged by [[Richard Marlow]], has also received airplay in some countries.

"Season Song", a song performed by British band [[Blue States (band)|Blue States]], from their 2002 album "Man Mountain", was released as a single, containing a remixed version by Ru Da Silva and a "Taxi" ([[Ave Maria (Gounod)|Ave Maria]]) remix by [[Jacknife Lee]]. "Season song" has been called one of the album's highlights.
